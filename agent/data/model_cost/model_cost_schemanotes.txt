Files and schema choices

1) openai.csv
   One row per priced OpenAI model/mode/context/modality slice.
   Why: OpenAI's official pricing mixes context bands, modalities, per-minute estimates, per-second video pricing,
   and fine-tuning training-hour pricing, so a row-oriented schema is the least lossy.

2) anthropic.csv
   One row per Anthropic model with separate standard prompt-caching columns and batch columns.
   Why: Anthropic's pricing is naturally model-centric and explicitly splits standard base input, cache write/read,
   and batch pricing.

3) google.csv
   One row per Google model and pricing mode.
   Why: Google's Gemini pricing varies by paid mode, modality, prompt-size band, and sometimes image/video/request units.
   The schema keeps the main token prices in columns and preserves extra pricing details in the
   context_cache_storage_or_other_usd and notes columns.
