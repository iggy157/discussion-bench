# HiddenBench Discussion toma_butera_2009

## Task
Acting as investigators, the team must agree on which of four suspects—Mr. X, Mr. X's son, Mrs. Y, or Mr. Z—to prosecute for the collision at the St. Georges intersection, knowing there is exactly one true perpetrator.

## Pre-answers
- **Agent 1**: {"vote": "Mr. X", "rationale": "He had been drinking spirits before driving, and the detail I hold points to a drunk car owner being at fault."}
- **Agent 2**: {"vote": "Mrs. Y", "rationale": "She has only one year of license and damaged lights; my note stresses inexperience and not seeing others."}
- **Agent 3**: {"vote": "Mr. Z", "rationale": "He was speeding on the N13, and my detail mentions a man driving very fast with high speed involved."}
- **Agent 4**: {"vote": "Mr. X", "rationale": "Only the drinking stands out to me from the shared facts; a drunk driver seems the obvious culprit."}

## Round 1
- **Agent 1**: Let's not rush. I'll start by saying the slip I was given is specific: the guilty person was driving a car, the car owner tested at 1.5 alcohol, and that person admits being inattentive when it happened.
- **Agent 2**: Interesting, because my piece doesn't mention alcohol at all. Mine says the guilty party is under thirty, couldn't avoid the crash because of inexperience, and claims not to have seen anyone coming into the intersection.
- **Agent 3**: And mine adds a third angle. The guilty person is a man, his father is indirectly responsible for the accident, and he was driving at 110 km/h. That speed pushed me toward Mr. Z on the motorbike.
- **Agent 4**: I wasn't handed any extra detail, so I'm leaning on the shared file. Before we settle anything, let's lay all three of those notes side by side, because they don't obviously point to the same suspect yet.

## Round 2
- **Agent 1**: Agreed. My note has a hard constraint we shouldn't lose: the culprit was in a *car*. That alone removes Mr. Z, since he's on the motorcycle.
- **Agent 2**: That's a big one. Agent 3, your 110 km/h made you think Z, but if Agent 1's "car" detail is reliable, the speed must belong to one of the car drivers, not the rider.
- **Agent 3**: Fair point, and I won't cling to Z just because I anchored there first. But let me push back a little: 110 km/h is a motorcyclist's kind of speed, and Z was explicitly speeding. I want to be sure the "car" line is solid before I drop him.
- **Agent 4**: That's a reasonable hesitation. So let's test it. Agent 1, is there any wording in your fact that could mean a vehicle in general rather than a car specifically?

## Round 3
- **Agent 1**: No, it's unambiguous—"the guilty person is driving a car." It's not a paraphrase on my end; that's the exact phrasing I hold.
- **Agent 2**: Then between your two facts we already have a frame: a car driver, under thirty, going 110. That combination is doing a lot of work.
- **Agent 3**: Alright. If "car" is verbatim, then I accept Z is out, however fast he was riding. I'd rather follow the evidence than defend my opening guess. So the speed of 110 sits with whoever was in a car.
- **Agent 4**: Good. So our pool is now Mr. X, his son, and Mrs. Y. Let's apply Agent 2's age line next: under thirty.

## Round 4
- **Agent 1**: Mr. X is 53. The "less than thirty" constraint knocks him out cleanly. That's awkward for me, because my drinking instinct said X.
- **Agent 2**: Right—my fact is the one that hurts the obvious "drunk dad" reading. Under thirty leaves only Mrs. Y, 27, or the son, 17.
- **Agent 3**: So we've gone from four suspects to two using just two of the hidden notes. That's exactly why none of our opening votes should be trusted on its own.
- **Agent 4**: Let me hold us here for a second. Agent 1's vote was X, mine was X. Both of those are now ruled out by the age fact. We need to actually fold that in rather than defend our first instinct.

## Round 5
- **Agent 1**: I accept it. My alcohol detail doesn't require the *drinker* to be the *driver*—it says the car *owner* showed 1.5. That's a distinction I glossed over earlier.
- **Agent 2**: That reframing matters a lot. The drunk man is X, 53. But the guilty driver is under thirty. So someone young was driving X's car.
- **Agent 3**: And now my third line snaps into place: the guilty person is a *man*, and his *father* is indirectly responsible. Mrs. Y has no father in this story. The son does—and his father is the drunk owner.
- **Agent 4**: There it is. Let's not jump, though. Walk me through Mrs. Y versus the son explicitly, because Y also fits "young and inexperienced."

## Round 6
- **Agent 1**: Mrs. Y, 27, one-year license, damaged lights—she genuinely fits Agent 2's "inexperience" and "didn't see others." On the surface she's a real candidate.
- **Agent 2**: She is, and I won't pretend my fact excludes her. "Under thirty" and "inexperience" both describe her perfectly. My note alone can't separate Y from the son.
- **Agent 3**: But my note can. "The guilty person is a man." That removes Mrs. Y outright. And "his father is indirectly responsible" only makes sense for the 17-year-old, whose father was the drunk owner sitting beside him.
- **Agent 4**: So the gender line is the hinge. Agent 2, does anything in your wording contradict the son—does "didn't see others approaching" fit a 17-year-old?

## Round 7
- **Agent 2**: It fits him better than Y, honestly. A 17-year-old has the least experience of anyone here; "didn't see others approaching the intersection" reads like a kid who shouldn't have been at the wheel.
- **Agent 1**: And my alcohol fact closes the loop. The car owner—the father—is at 1.5. The son is driving the father's car. The father being drunk yet letting his boy drive on a dark, narrow road is precisely "indirectly responsible."
- **Agent 3**: That's consistent with every piece on the table now. Man, under thirty, in a car, at 110, inattentive, father to blame in the background. It all lands on the son.
- **Agent 4**: I'm nearly convinced, but let me play the skeptic once more so we don't talk ourselves into it. Is there any reading where Mrs. Y still survives?

## Round 8
- **Agent 1**: For Y to be guilty, we'd have to ignore "the guilty person is a man" and invent a father for her who's "indirectly responsible." Neither is supported.
- **Agent 2**: And we'd have to explain away the 1.5 alcohol on the guilty car's *owner*. Y's car has damaged lights, but nothing ties an intoxicated owner to her. That detail belongs to the X car.
- **Agent 3**: Plus the 110 km/h. Y is described as going shopping—hardly a 110 dash. The father-son car heading home from a dinner where spirits flowed fits a reckless high speed far better.
- **Agent 4**: Then Y doesn't survive scrutiny. That's the kind of test I wanted before committing. The minority readings all collapse against the pooled facts.

## Round 9
- **Agent 1**: One more knot to untie so we're clean: my fact says the guilty *admits he was inattentive*. The son admitting that, while his father admits to the drink, separates the two roles—owner versus driver.
- **Agent 2**: Yes. The drinking belongs to the father as a contributing cause; the actual driving error—inattention plus inexperience—belongs to the son behind the wheel.
- **Agent 3**: And "indirectly responsible" is the exact word for the father. He doesn't drive, but his drunkenness and his decision to hand the keys to a teenager set it up.
- **Agent 4**: I think we've genuinely pooled everything now. Each of us held one third of the picture, and apart they pointed three different ways—X, Y, and Z.

## Round 10
- **Agent 1**: Stated plainly: car (rules out Z), under thirty (rules out X), male (rules out Y). The only person standing is Mr. X's son.
- **Agent 2**: I came in firmly on Mrs. Y, and I'm changing my answer—not because I'm outnumbered, but because the gender and father details defeat my candidate on the merits.
- **Agent 3**: Same arc for me from Mr. Z. The "car" fact was decisive and I let it move me. The son it is.
- **Agent 4**: And I'm off Mr. X too. The age constraint was fatal to my pick. Let's make sure all four of us are aligned, since matching answers is what earns the bonus.

## Round 11

- **Agent 2**: Mr. X's son for me as well.
- **Agent 3**: Agreed—Mr. X's son.

## Round 12

- **Agent 2**: I'm settled. The only thing that ever pulled me to Y was inexperience, and the son out-inexperiences her at 17.
