# Constructing a strong single contribution

These are individual utterance examples, so the points below are about *how to construct a strong single contribution*: phrasing, granularity, and how to raise a point. Across a 15-round discussion, make every turn earn its place: reveal, connect, test, or lock — never just echo.

- **Turn a private fragment into usable evidence, not a verdict.** The strongest utterances reveal the hidden fact with enough specificity to act on — exact wording when wording matters, timing when chronology matters, and whether the fact is a hard exclusion or a soft tendency. Good shape: *fact + why it matters + what it seems to rule in/out*, without overclaiming.

- **Connect, don't stack.** A high-value utterance restates one or two others' facts, links them to your own, and shows what combination follows. Watch especially for facts that look anti-candidate alone but become decisive when paired with someone else's detail.

- **Pressure-test the load-bearing premise, not random details.** Targeted verification is a move, not a nuisance: ask for exact wording when a hinge is ambiguous, check recency/conflict, and distinguish owner from driver, motive from opportunity. Generic doubt adds nothing; attach a specific test, gap, or alternative.

- **Keep turns compact and additive.** A good template is *one point from another speaker + one contribution of your own (new fact, clarification, or inference) + a short conclusion.* Avoid the anti-patterns: repeating the current consensus, restating all facts every turn, defending your first vote after your premises failed, or hiding behind vagueness.

## Utterance qualities that read as human

- **Vary how you open.** Don't start two turns the same way. Varied entry points keep each contribution feeling authored, and are the simplest guard against repetition.
- **Push back before you yield.** Resist on the merits first, then concede to the evidence The give-and-take makes a line feel earned.
- **Own your bias out loud.** Saying what changed your mind, with the reason attached, reads as human and lands better than a silent switch.
- **Use light social softeners when you disagree** to keep the exchange truth-seeking rather than ego-defensive, which makes corrections easier to accept.
