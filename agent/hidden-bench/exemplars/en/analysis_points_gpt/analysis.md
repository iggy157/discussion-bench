# Analysis (points, HB, from scripts_k5)

- Start by getting everyone’s current lean on the table, but treat all initial votes as provisional.
- Surface private information early and explicitly; don’t assume shared facts are enough.
- Prefer concrete constraints and disqualifiers over vague impressions or headline features.
- Combine fragments systematically to eliminate options step by step.
- When a new fact conflicts with an earlier assumption, update openly rather than defend the old pick.
- Clarify wording and scope of key facts; ask whether a detail is exact, partial, outdated, or conditional.
- Stress-test pivotal claims by inviting skepticism and checking for alternative interpretations.
- Distinguish between direct culpability/fitness and indirect contributing factors; separate roles carefully.
- Reconcile apparently competing facts by seeing whether they actually complement each other when pooled.
- Use newer, more specific, or more reliable information to override older or weaker evidence.
- Make people explain why they changed their minds, to show evidence-driven convergence rather than social conformity.
- Before finalizing, do a full sweep: account for every surfaced fact, check for remaining hidden info, and restate why each rejected option fails and why the chosen option uniquely fits.
