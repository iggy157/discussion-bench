# Analysis (points, HB, from scripts_k5)

### Key Points for High-Quality Collective Discussion

*   **Prioritize Information Pooling:** Before committing to a final answer, ensure every agent explicitly shares their private fragments. Avoid treating initial votes as anchors; treat them as hypotheses to be tested.
*   **Deconstruct "Obvious" Conclusions:** Actively challenge surface-level evidence or "obvious" candidates. Use private data to uncover hidden contradictions that override the shared general information.
*   **Sequential Constraint Application:** Apply constraints (e.g., age, gender, location, timing) one by one to systematically narrow the pool of candidates rather than jumping to a conclusion.
*   **Verify Verbatim Phrasing:** When a specific detail is pivotal, verify the exact wording of the private note to ensure the conclusion isn't based on a paraphrase or a misinterpretation.
*   **Steelmann Opposing Views:** Deliberately argue in favor of discarded options or minority views to ensure they are truly invalidated by the evidence and not just outvoted.
*   **Cross-Reference and Synthesize:** Look for "converging webs" where separate fragments from different agents reinforce a single narrative or explain each other's anomalies.
*   **Avoid Momentum-Based Agreement:** Explicitly resist "stampeding" toward a consensus. Require agents to state their reasons for changing their minds based on specific evidence rather than group tilt.
*   **Conduct a Final Evidence Audit:** Before locking in the answer, perform a comprehensive sweep to ensure every surfaced fact has been accounted for and nothing contradicts the final choice.
*   **Distinguish Roles and Nuances:** Pay close attention to subtle distinctions in the data (e.g., "owner" vs. "driver," "main kitchen" vs. "cold prep") to avoid oversimplifying the facts.
*   **Formalize the Reasoning Chain:** Conclude by articulating the full logical path—from the initial pool through each elimination—to ensure the final consensus is transparent and robust.
