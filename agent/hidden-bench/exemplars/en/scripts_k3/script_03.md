# HiddenBench Discussion evacuate_park_dilemma

## Task
With thunderstorms closing in, the team must urgently tell all festival-goers which of three evacuation sites—Blueberry Ridge, Green Valley, or Red Lake—is the single safe, accessible, and prepared option.

## Pre-answers
- **Agent 1**: {"vote": "Blueberry Ridge", "rationale": "It has generators and a medical station, and it's already prepping rooms—best equipped on paper, and the Green Valley signs make me hesitant to send people there."}
- **Agent 2**: {"vote": "Blueberry Ridge", "rationale": "A medical station matters most in a storm with injuries possible; roads were declared passable this morning."}
- **Agent 3**: {"vote": "Red Lake", "rationale": "It has handled large festival crowds before and got bulk food today, so it can absorb the most people."}
- **Agent 4**: {"vote": "Red Lake", "rationale": "Proven capacity for overflow plus fresh supplies; seems the safest bet for sheer numbers."}

## Round 1
- **Agent 1**: Let's lay out where we each lean before we commit. I'm drawn to Blueberry Ridge for the medical station and generators, but I want to stress-test that. The Green Valley warning signs bother me.
- **Agent 2**: I'm with you on Blueberry Ridge. If anyone gets hurt in the chaos, having medical capacity on site could save lives. The morning road inspection covers all three, so access seems fine across the board.
- **Agent 3**: I went the other way—Red Lake. It's hosted overflow crowds before and took in bulk food this morning. For thousands of people, capacity might beat amenities.
- **Agent 4**: Same instinct here, Red Lake. But I'll flag now that I'm holding a piece of information that complicates things. Let me hear the round out before I drop it, so we don't anchor too fast.

## Round 2
- **Agent 1**: Good call, Agent 4—don't sit on anything that matters. The part I was given actually changes how I read Green Valley. Officials confirmed the pest closure applies only to tourists; the site stays open for emergency evacuations, and they've sent extra staff in.
- **Agent 2**: Huh. So the warning signs we all saw are essentially a red herring for our purposes?
- **Agent 3**: That's a meaningful reframe. I'd written Green Valley off entirely because of those signs. If it's staffed up specifically for emergencies, it can't be dismissed.
- **Agent 4**: Agreed, hold that thought. Now mine: an emergency text alert just went out—a sinkhole opened across the only road to Red Lake, and law enforcement has closed the route. That's the piece I didn't want us to skip past.

## Round 3
- **Agent 1**: That's decisive against Red Lake, isn't it? Supplies don't help if no vehicle can reach the hall.
- **Agent 2**: It directly contradicts the morning inspection. The roads were "passable this morning," but the sinkhole appeared in today's storm. Newer information wins.
- **Agent 3**: I have to step back from my own pick, then. My Red Lake reasoning was all about capacity, and a closed road erases capacity. I'm not defending it out of pride—the closure is the deciding fact.
- **Agent 4**: Right. So we're down to Blueberry Ridge versus Green Valley. Let's not rush to Blueberry just because two of us liked it earlier. What else is on the table?

## Round 4
- **Agent 1**: Before we lean Blueberry, I want to hear from Agent 3, who hasn't shared a held fact yet.
- **Agent 2**: Same. We've each apparently got one piece. Agent 3?
- **Agent 3**: Mine is exactly the catch. All power at Blueberry Ridge failed midday—including the backups. The medical equipment is unusable, and the lodge is only accepting people who can walk miles to reach it.
- **Agent 4**: That guts the entire case for Blueberry Ridge. The generators and medical station were the whole reason it looked strong.

## Round 5
- **Agent 1**: So the appeal of Blueberry was the one thing that's now broken. And "only accepting those who can walk miles" is a non-starter for evacuating a festival crowd with kids, elderly, and possibly injured people.
- **Agent 2**: I'll own the shift—I voted Blueberry twice on the medical angle. With no power and no working equipment, that rationale collapses. I'm not holding the position just to be consistent.
- **Agent 3**: And there's still my own fact to weigh too. Walking miles in a thunderstorm is a hazard in itself, not a solution.
- **Agent 4**: Now let me add mine, because it compounds this. The bridge over Pine Creek—a key access point to Blueberry Ridge—collapsed in today's storm. Vehicle access to the lodge is cut off.

## Round 6
- **Agent 1**: So we have two independent reasons Blueberry fails: it can't function without power, and you largely can't drive there anyway.
- **Agent 2**: The "walk miles" detail and the bridge collapse fit together neatly—people are being told to walk because vehicles can't cross Pine Creek. Two facts, one consistent picture.
- **Agent 3**: That cross-checks well. When separate pieces of information explain each other like that, I trust them more.
- **Agent 4**: Let me tally where we stand. Red Lake: road closed by sinkhole. Blueberry Ridge: no power, equipment dead, bridge out, foot access only. Green Valley: open for emergencies, extra staff, prepping cots and blankets.

## Round 7
- **Agent 1**: Stated plainly like that, Green Valley is the only site that's both reachable and operational.
- **Agent 2**: I want to be careful, though—we just dismissed two options fast. Let me play devil's advocate on Green Valley so we're not picking it by elimination alone. What's the strongest objection?
- **Agent 3**: The pest outbreak and the posted warning signs. On the surface that screams "unsafe."
- **Agent 4**: But Agent 1's fact addresses that head-on. Agent 1, can you restate exactly what the officials confirmed?

## Round 8
- **Agent 1**: Yes. The closure is for tourists only. Local officials explicitly confirmed Green Valley remains open for emergency evacuations, and they've sent extra staff specifically for this. The signs are about routine visitors, not an emergency shelter operation.
- **Agent 2**: That satisfies my objection. A pest issue serious enough to bar leisure tourists is still being judged acceptable by the same officials for sheltering people from a storm—and they staffed up rather than locked down.
- **Agent 3**: And the shared briefing already told us the main building is preparing cots and handing out blankets. That's active readiness, not just a sign on a fence.
- **Agent 4**: So the warning signs were the thing that almost steered all of us away from the right answer. Good that we surfaced the correction.

## Round 9
- **Agent 1**: Let me also double-check access to Green Valley, since access is what sank the other two. Does anyone hold anything negative about the route there?
- **Agent 2**: Nothing from me. My fact was the Red Lake sinkhole.
- **Agent 3**: Mine was the Blueberry power failure. No road issue for Green Valley.
- **Agent 4**: And mine was the Pine Creek bridge to Blueberry. I've got nothing against Green Valley's road. So the only verified hazards we hold all point at the other two sites.

## Round 10
- **Agent 1**: Then the morning "passable" inspection still stands for Green Valley, unchallenged by any later alert.
- **Agent 2**: That's the key asymmetry. For Red Lake and Blueberry, newer information overrode the morning report. For Green Valley, nothing has overridden it.
- **Agent 3**: Capacity question, though—Green Valley is a campground center. Can it hold a festival's worth of people?
- **Agent 4**: Fair concern. But cots and blankets are being distributed and extra staff are on site. It may not be Red Lake's proven overflow capacity, but it's the only place that's actually open and reachable, so capacity comparisons with closed sites are moot.

## Round 11
- **Agent 1**: Right—an imperfect open shelter beats a perfect inaccessible one. Maximizing people evacuated safely means sending them somewhere they can physically arrive and be received.
- **Agent 2**: I'm fully off Blueberry now. The medical argument I championed is dead without power, and people can't drive there.
- **Agent 3**: And I'm off Red Lake; the closed road ends it. I'd rather be persuaded by the alert than cling to my first answer.
- **Agent 4**: So all four of us have moved off our pre-discussion picks based on what got pooled. That's the system working.

## Round 12
- **Agent 1**: Let me make sure we haven't ignored any minority worry. Agent 3, you raised capacity—are you satisfied, or do you still want a hedge?
- **Agent 3**: I'm satisfied. My worry only mattered if a higher-capacity site were available, and none is reachable. I withdraw the objection on the merits, not because I'm outvoted.
- **Agent 2**: One operational note: we should advise people clearly that Green Valley is the designated emergency shelter despite the public warning signs, or they'll second-guess and scatter.
- **Agent 4**: Strong point. The messaging has to preempt the sign confusion explicitly.

## Round 13
- **Agent 1**: Let me restate our reasoning chain so nothing's resting on a single source. Red Lake: sinkhole closed the only road—Agent 2's alert. Blueberry: power and backups out plus bridge collapse—Agent 3 and Agent 4. Green Valley: open for emergencies with extra staff—Agent 1—and already prepping cots, from the shared brief.
- **Agent 2**: That holds together. Each elimination rests on a specific, recent piece, and each piece came from a different one of us.
- **Agent 3**: No contradictions among the facts, and the two Blueberry facts actually reinforce each other. I'm confident.
- **Agent 4**: Then the recommendation is Green Valley, with clear instructions that it's the official emergency site regardless of the tourist warning signage.

## Round 14
- **Agent 1**: Agreed. I want our advisory to add: do not attempt Blueberry Ridge on foot—the "walkable" framing could lure people into a dangerous trek with no working medical support.
- **Agent 2**: Yes, and explicitly say the Red Lake route is closed by law enforcement so nobody tries it from old directions.
- **Agent 3**: And reassure people the Green Valley pest closure does not apply to evacuees—extra staff are waiting. That removes the hesitation.
- **Agent 4**: Good. Direct, specific, and it accounts for every hazard we surfaced.

## Round 15
- **Agent 1**: Final check—any dissent, any nagging doubt? Speak now.
- **Agent 2**: None. The evidence converges cleanly on Green Valley.
- **Agent 3**: Agreed, and I'm glad we didn't lock in on Blueberry early. The medical station looked decisive until the power fact landed.
- **Agent 4**: Settled, then. Green Valley is our unanimous recommendation, with the messaging caveats we listed. Let's send it.
