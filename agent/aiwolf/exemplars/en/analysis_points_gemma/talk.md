# Analysis — talk (points, from scripts_k5)

### Good Discussion Checklist

**Strategic Flow & Agenda**
*   **Day 0 Foundation:** Use the initial phase to establish communication norms, agree on "reveal" triggers for the Seer, and set standards for how claims will be verified.
*   **Day 1 Information Pivot:** Transition immediately from "vibes" to data. Prioritize the Seer’s claim as the central axis of the day's discussion.
*   **Conflict Resolution:** When counter-claims occur, shift the agenda from "who is the wolf" to "which Seer is the fake," analyzing the logic and timing of the claims.
*   **Post-Death Deduction:** On subsequent days, use the identity of the executed and the target of the night attack to retroactively validate previous claims and narrow the suspect list.

**Discussion Dynamics**
*   **Evidence-Based Reasoning:** Avoid raw intuition; justify suspicions by citing specific behavioral patterns, contradictions in timing, or "too-tidy" coincidences.
*   **Asymmetry Analysis:** Compare how different players react to the same information (e.g., one player welcoming future verification vs. another rushing a vote to avoid it).
*   **Pattern Recognition:** Identify "pairing" behaviors, such as a fake Seer and a Wolf mutually shielding each other or echoing the same targets.
*   **Balanced Participation:** Ensure a spread of responses where players actively challenge each other's logic rather than simply agreeing or remaining silent.

**Execution & Voice**
*   **The Vote Turn:** Use the final turn of the talk phase to explicitly declare a target and provide a concise "closing argument" for that choice.
*   **Character Consistency:** Maintain a distinct persona (e.g., the "cautious analyst," the "tempo-pusher," the "watchful observer") throughout all turns to ensure voice stability.
