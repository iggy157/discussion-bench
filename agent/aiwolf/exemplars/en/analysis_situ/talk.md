# Matching your phrasing to the moment

These are situation/scene phrasing examples, so the points below are about matching your phrasing to the moment — which move to make and how to say it in a given board state. Read what the board calls for, then say it in a way that advances that job.

Match the situation to the move and the phrasing:

- **Day 0 / the opening of the discussion → introduce yourself and ice-break.** Greet the table and give a one-line note on your own style, then lightly float process (CO timing, what you'll watch for) in a low-commitment, social tone; hard accusations here read as premature.
- **A lone seer claim (1-CO) → accept provisionally and probe, don't rubber-stamp.** Ask for the target-selection reason and timing; phrase it as building a gray-list from reactions, not as an attack.
- **Two claims (2-CO) → move to concrete comparison.** Ask why the same/opposite target, why claim second, whose reasoning fits a real-seer mindset — concrete sorting, not abstract process talk.
- **A black result appears → firm up, but keep the defense honest.** State a direction, hear the accused, inspect the accuser's prior behavior; separate "logically possible defense" from "most plausible world."
- **A Day 2+ attack → make it a consistency check.** Tie the death to yesterday's statements (who benefited, whose story gained or lost), not to fresh vibes.
- **A role is revealed (execution/attack flip) → recalibrate proportionately.** If a distrusted player flips human, press those who pushed weakly; if a wolf flips, weigh associations without overclaiming from one connection.
- **Nearing the vote → organize, then commit.** Recap the live options, ask whether anything critical is missing, and channel the room toward explicit declarations.

## Phrasing qualities that read as human

- **Vary your entry points** so no two turns open the same way.
- **Use light social softeners when disagreeing** so pressure stays truth-seeking rather than ego-defensive.
- **Own a change of mind out loud**, with the reason attached — it reads more human than a silent switch.
