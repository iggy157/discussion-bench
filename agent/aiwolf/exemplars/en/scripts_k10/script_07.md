## Day 0

### Talk
#### Turn 0
- **Olivia**: Morning, all. Olivia here. No reads yet, obviously — let's set a calm tone and actually use our turns well.
- **Marcus**: Hey everyone, Marcus. Happy to play it straight. Five of us, one wolf — patience beats panic, so let's not flail.
- **Priya**: Priya. I'll mostly listen today and track who's pushing whom. The wolf usually leaks through tempo, not words.
- **Dorian**: Dorian, delighted to be here! I like a loud table. Quiet players make me nervous, so speak up, friends.
- **Felix**: Felix. I'm blunt, so brace yourselves. Day zero is cheap talk; I care who claims what once we get real info.

#### Turn 1
- **Olivia**: Practical question: when should our seer come out? I'd say Day 1, early. Hiding a result helps only the wolf.
- **Marcus**: Agreed on an early claim. If the seer sits on a wolf read overnight, we waste the whole first day guessing.
- **Priya**: Noted. If there's a result worth sharing, it gets shared Day 1, full reasoning attached. No coy half-claims.
- **Dorian**: Bold of you all to assume the seer survives the night. Still — yes, claim early, let the rest of us pile on.
- **Felix**: Fine. Then today's only job is baselines. Marcus, you jumped to agree with Olivia fast. I'm logging that.

#### Turn 2
- **Olivia**: @Felix that's a fair flag, but agreement isn't a crime. I'll judge people on Day 1 votes, not Day 0 manners.
- **Marcus**: I agreed because she's right, Felix, not to hide. If echoing good ideas is wolfy, half the village is doomed.
- **Priya**: I like that Felix is already building a ledger. Keep it. I'm watching who over-explains nothing in particular.
- **Dorian**: I'll be honest — I have strong instincts and I trust them more than logs. We'll see who reality vindicates.
- **Felix**: Instincts without evidence are just vibes, Dorian. Bring numbers tomorrow. Over for me today.

#### Turn 3
- **Olivia**: Nothing more from me. Rested and ready for real information. Over.
- **Marcus**: Same. Sleep well, and let's keep our heads tomorrow. Over.
- **Priya**: I'll close here and let the night speak. Over.
- **Dorian**: Saving my fireworks for Day 1. Over.
- **Felix**: Over.

### Night
- divine: Priya -> Marcus (werewolf)

## Day 1

### Talk
#### Turn 0
- **Olivia**: Everyone made it. So no attack landed yet, which is unusual but fine. Seer, this is your cue — claim now.
- **Marcus**: Glad we're all here. Let's hear the seer before anyone starts swinging. I want the result and the logic.
- **Priya**: Seer, confirming. Last night I divined Marcus and he came up WEREWOLF. I'm asking the table to execute him today.
- **Dorian**: Well, this is awkward — because I'm the seer, and I divined Marcus as HUMAN. I also checked Felix: WEREWOLF.
- **Felix**: Two seers. Great. One of you is lying, and I happen to know I'm not a wolf — so Dorian, your read on me is fake.

#### Turn 1
- **Olivia**: Okay, slow down — two claims, let's actually compare them instead of grabbing the nearest torch. Lay it out.
- **Marcus**: Conveniently, both seers are talking about me. Priya brands me, Dorian clears me. I say Priya's the wolf, frankly.
- **Priya**: Of course the wolf says that. Note the shape: Dorian's claim doesn't just clear Marcus, it adds a second target. Felix.
- **Dorian**: Because Felix IS the wolf! My result stands. Marcus is clean, and Priya is throwing him to cover her own trail.
- **Felix**: Dorian, I'm a villager. I know my own card. Your Felix-result is invented, which makes your Marcus-clear worthless too.

#### Turn 2
- **Olivia**: Let me think out loud. If Dorian were the true seer, why fabricate a wolf where Felix sits? Felix gains nothing lying about being plain village.
- **Marcus**: Or Felix and Priya are teamed and Dorian caught it. You're all converging on me too easily — that's the trap.
- **Priya**: There's no team of two; one wolf, remember. So among Marcus, Dorian, Felix, exactly one of those claims is wolf-built.
- **Dorian**: Then execute Felix and find out! If I'm wrong, you've lost a villager. If Priya's wrong, you've handed the wolf a free day.
- **Felix**: Wrong — execute Marcus and you either kill the wolf or expose the liar instantly. My card clears the Felix line entirely.

#### Turn 3
- **Olivia**: I'm convinced. Felix flatly knows his own role, which kills Dorian's second result — and a fake second result means a fake clear of Marcus. Priya reads true to me. @Marcus
- **Marcus**: You're railroading. I'm human and you'll regret burning me, but I can't stop five chairs. For the record, I think Priya's your wolf. @Felix
- **Priya**: Thank you, Olivia. The logic holds: my single, clean call on Marcus beats a two-target story Felix already disproved. @Marcus
- **Dorian**: You're all sleepwalking into Priya's hands, and I'll say so to the end — the wolf is Felix, not Marcus. @Felix
- **Felix**: One wolf, one liar, and the liar invented a wolf out of me. That points straight through Marcus. @Marcus

### Vote
- Olivia -> Marcus
- Marcus -> Felix
- Priya -> Marcus
- Dorian -> Felix
- Felix -> Marcus
- execution: Marcus (werewolf)

### Night
- divine: Priya -> Dorian (human)
- attack: (no werewolf remaining)

- winner: village
- The table refused to rush, weighed both seer claims, and Felix's honest self-read collapsed the fake. Marcus the werewolf was executed Day 1, and the village won.
