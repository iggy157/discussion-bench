# Analysis (where to look)

### Withholding or Poorly Timed Disclosure
- Participants establish a shared expectation for when specific types of private information should be revealed to maximize its utility.
- Disclosures are timed to either resolve a deadlock or provide a necessary pivot for the group's reasoning.

### Premature Convergence
- The group explicitly identifies and tests alternative hypotheses before committing to a final conclusion.
- A deliberate pause is maintained to ensure that all available evidence has been integrated into the current theory.

### Reflexive Conformity
- Participants actively seek out and weigh dissenting perspectives before aligning with the majority.
- Individual reasoning is articulated independently of the prevailing consensus to prevent cognitive mirroring.

### Stagnation and Low Diversity
- Discussion shifts toward new lines of inquiry whenever the current dialogue becomes repetitive or circular.
- Participants introduce novel angles of analysis to challenge the existing framework of the conversation.

### Weak Verification
- Claims are systematically cross-referenced against previously established facts for internal consistency.
- The underlying logic or evidence supporting a conclusion is requested and scrutinized before the conclusion is accepted.
