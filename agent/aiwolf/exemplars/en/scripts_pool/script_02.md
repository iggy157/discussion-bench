- **Theo**: Sure, sure. I just don't want us so cautious we waste the day. I'll push tempo so we don't stall.
- **Grace**: Tempo's fine, Theo, but pushing people is also how a wolf hides in the noise. I'll be watching how hard you push.

- **Theo**: Deal. And I'll hold you to it right back, Iris. Looking forward to it.

#### Turn 3

- **Iris**: Over.
- **Theo**: Over — see you all tomorrow.
- **Grace**: Over.

## Day 1

### Talk
#### Turn 0
- **Mara**: Morning. No one died — Day 0 has no attack, as expected. Seer, the floor's yours. Let's hear it.
- **Dolph**: Yeah, all five of us still here. Seer, please claim with your result and your reasoning, like we agreed.
- **Iris**: Then I'll lead. I'm the seer. Last night I divined Mara — the result was human. So Mara is cleared in my book.
- **Theo**: That's a problem, because I'm the seer. And I divined Mara too — she came up werewolf. One of us is lying, Iris.
- **Grace**: Two seers, opposite results on the same target. So exactly one of you is real, and the other is wolf or possessed.

#### Turn 1
- **Mara**: Convenient that I'm the contested one. I'm a plain villager — Iris read me right. Theo's "werewolf" on me is just a frame.
- **Dolph**: Or Iris cleared you to build trust, Mara. Theo calling you wolf can't be dismissed that fast. I lean toward checking you hard.
- **Iris**: Think it through. If Theo were real, why burn his Night-0 read on a quiet villager? Mara played calm and normal. I stand by human.
- **Theo**: I targeted Mara precisely because she's quiet — quiet wolves win. Iris is improvising. Ask her why she "happened" to pick Mara too.
- **Grace**: Honestly, two seers landing on the same name is suspicious for the FAKE — it's easy to mirror a real claim to muddy the water.

#### Turn 2
- **Mara**: Grace nails it. The fake mirrors the real seer's target to turn it into my-word-against-yours. And notice who's amplifying it.
- **Dolph**: Meaning me? I'm just not ready to clear you on Iris's say-so. That's not wolfy, that's careful.
- **Iris**: It's a little wolfy, Dolph. You skipped the seer puzzle entirely and jumped to "lynch the cleared villager." That only helps a hidden wolf.
- **Theo**: Don't let Iris reframe this. The cleanest play is simple: vote Mara, my werewolf read. If I'm right, the game's basically over.
- **Grace**: No — if Iris is real, lynching Mara wastes a day and kills a villager. Safer to resolve the seer war first. One of you two goes.

#### Turn 3
- **Mara**: Between the seers, Theo's story leans on me being a "quiet wolf," yet Dolph's the one steering us off the seer fight. I trust Iris. @Theo
- **Dolph**: I think you're all misreading this. Iris cleared a contested player out of nowhere; that's the real tell. I'm not folding. @Iris
- **Iris**: I gave my reasoning and Theo gave a slogan. Resolve the fakes — vote the seer claim that doesn't hold up. @Theo
- **Theo**: You're walking into it. When Mara flips wolf tomorrow, remember I told you. @Iris
- **Grace**: Theo's pressure all day reads like someone who needs a fast kill before we think. I'm with Mara and Iris. @Theo

### Vote
- Mara -> Theo
- Dolph -> Iris
- Iris -> Theo
- Theo -> Iris
- Grace -> Theo
- execution: Theo (POSSESSED)
