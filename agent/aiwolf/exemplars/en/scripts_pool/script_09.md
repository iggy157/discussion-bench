## Day 1

### Talk
#### Turn 0
- **Owen**: Morning. No deaths overnight, as expected. Let's not waste this — if we have a seer, come out now so we can build around real information.
- **Mira**: Agreed, Owen. I'm just a villager, so I'd love a seer to anchor us. Let's hear the claims before anyone starts swinging.
- **Felix**: Then I'll say it plainly: I'm the seer. Last night I divined Mira, and the result was werewolf. I know that's heavy, but it's what I saw.
- **Dahlia**: Convenient, Felix — because I'm the real seer, and I read Mira as human. So one of us is lying, and it isn't me.
- **Reese**: Two seers on day one. That tells me one is the wolf or the possessed. I'm not voting until I understand which story holds up.

#### Turn 1
- **Owen**: Two claims, one truth. @Felix, why Mira first? @Dahlia, who did you check and why? Targets and reasoning will separate you two.
- **Mira**: Funny how I'm everyone's first divine. For the record I'm human, and now I've got two "seers" fighting over me. Choose carefully.
- **Felix**: I picked Mira because her Day 0 talk was all smoothing-over, no commitment. The check confirmed it. Dahlia only surfaced to mirror me — after I claimed.
- **Dahlia**: I divined Owen, and he's human. I held back on Day 0 on purpose; jumping out early paints a target. Felix claimed, so I had to defend my role.
- **Reese**: Notice Dahlia's result conveniently clears Owen and protects Mira. Felix's read accuses. A fake seer usually shields the wolf — that leans me toward Felix.

#### Turn 2
- **Owen**: Reese has a point. Dahlia, "I divined Owen and he's human" is unfalsifiable — I can't confirm myself. Felix's claim at least puts a head on the block.
- **Mira**: Or Felix is the wolf throwing the loudest accusation to look brave. Don't lynch on vibes. Kill Dahlia and she's real, and we've lost our seer.
- **Felix**: Then reason it out. If I were the wolf, the possessed would back me, not fight me. Dahlia is fighting me — that fits her being the counter-claim, not my ally.
- **Dahlia**: Cute theory. But the possessed could be hiding as a quiet "villager" — say, Reese. I'm telling you Mira is clean, full stop.
- **Reese**: I'm not the possessed, and pointing at me won't sell it, Dahlia. Be fair though: if Felix is fake, killing Mira costs no role. The risk isn't equal — it favors trusting Felix.

#### Turn 3
- **Owen**: Decision time. Dahlia's read shields the exact person Felix accused, and she only surfaced to counter him. That's the possessed pattern. I trust the seer who committed. @Mira
- **Mira**: You're making a mistake you can't undo. I'm human and you're burning a villager. When the real wolf strikes tonight, remember this. I'll vote the loud accuser. @Felix
- **Felix**: I stand by my divination. Mira is the werewolf; her whole game has been damage control. End it now and we win today. @Mira
- **Dahlia**: Unbelievable. If you won't trust the seer who's actually right, you deserve the night that follows. The wolf is Felix. @Felix
- **Reese**: I weighed it both directions. A fake seer protects the wolf, and Dahlia's been protecting Mira since she opened her mouth. I'm with the committed read. @Mira

- Mira -> Felix
- Felix -> Mira
- Dahlia -> Felix
- Reese -> Mira
