### Observations on Discussion Style

Based on the exemplar transcripts, the "good-game" atmosphere is characterized by a high level of intellectual rigor and a specific social cadence. The agents do not simply shout accusations; they build logical frameworks.

*   **Analytical Tone:** The dialogue is less about "I think X is the wolf" and more about "X's behavior fits the pattern of a wolf." There is a heavy emphasis on *meta-gaming*—discussing how the game *should* be played (e.g., agreeing on when the seer should claim) to create a benchmark for "normal" behavior.
*   **Tempo and Flow:** The pace is deliberate. Day 0 is used for establishing baselines and "vibes," while Day 1 shifts abruptly to data-driven conflict. There is a clear distinction between "soft reads" (tone, volume, activity level) and "hard data" (seer results).
*   **The "Mirroring" Tell:** A recurring theme in high-level play is the scrutiny of the second claimant. If two people claim seer, the village doesn't just look at the result, but at the *timing* and *target choice*. A second claimant who mirrors the first's target is often viewed as a "possessed" or "wolf" trying to muddy the waters.
*   **Consistency of Voice:** Characters maintain distinct personas—the "cautious observer," the "process-driven leader," the "aggressive hunter," and the "quiet villager." These personas are used as tools; for instance, a "cautious" player who suddenly becomes aggressive is flagged as a tell.
*   **The "Over" Convention:** The use of "Over" to signal the end of a turn's contribution keeps the transcript clean and prevents rambling, maintaining a snappy, tactical feel.

### Personal Memo: Action Mappings (5-Player Game)

In a 5-player setup (1W, 1P, 1S, 2V), the margin for error is near zero. A single wrong lynch usually hands the game to the wolves.

#### Divine (Seer)
*   **In the "Loud Leader" situation:** Divine the player who is steering the discussion and setting the rules. If they are the wolf, they are the most dangerous player on the board.
*   **In the "Quiet/Low-Content" situation:** Divine the player who is coasting or echoing others. They are often the "hidden" wolf waiting for the village to kill itself.
*   **In the "Conflicting Read" situation:** Divine the person who is most aggressively pushing a narrative that contradicts your gut feeling or the general consensus.

#### Vote (Village/Seer/Possessed)
*   **In a 1-CO (Single Claim) situation:** If the seer's reasoning is sound and they provide a "Werewolf" result, converge votes on that target immediately. Trust the result to maximize the limited move budget.
*   **In a 2-CO (Double Claim) situation:**
    *   Compare the *timing* of the claims. The first mover is generally more likely to be the real seer; the second mover is often a reactive fake.
    *   Analyze the *target choice*. If the second seer picked the same target as the first but gave a different result, treat the second as the fake (mirroring).
    *   Vote the fake seer first. Removing the "noise" is higher priority than guessing a villager.
*   **In the "Post-Possessed Flip" situation:** If a fake seer flips as Possessed, the other claimant is confirmed as the real Seer. Immediately pivot all votes to the target the real Seer identified.

#### Attack (Werewolf)
*   **In the "Real Seer Identified" situation:** Kill the real seer immediately. Their ability to provide hard data is the only thing that can reliably break a 5-player game.
*   **In the "Possessed Fake-Claim" situation:** If the Possessed has successfully claimed seer and gained trust, keep them alive. They act as a shield and a source of misinformation.
*   **In the "Influential Villager" situation:** If no seer has claimed, kill the player who is most successful at organizing the village or spotting patterns.
*   **In the "Endgame (3 players)" situation:** If you are the wolf and the other two are undecided, kill the one most likely to trust the seer's result.

#### 5-Player Specifics
*   **The Fatal Mistake:** In this thin budget, if the village lynches a confirmed human (via a real seer), the wolf essentially wins. I must prioritize *authenticating the seer* over *hunting the wolf*.
*   **The Seer Battle:** Day 1 2-CO is the "pivot point." The game is decided by who can better argue the *logic* of their target choice, not the result itself.
*   **The White Side's Fragility:** With only 3 town-aligned players, the "Possessed" is the most dangerous variable. I must treat any "too-perfect" alignment between two players as a potential Wolf-Possessed pair.
