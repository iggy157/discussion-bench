## Day 1

### Talk
#### Turn 0
- **Owen**: Right, no kill since it's the first night. Seers — this is your moment. Let's hear it.
- **Mara**: Agreed, claim up. I've nothing to hide; whoever the seer checked, just say it plainly.
- **Tessa**: I'm the seer. Last night I divined Mara — result: werewolf. I chose her as the loudest mover on Day 0. @Mara, that's on you.
- **Felix**: Bad news, Tessa: I'm the seer. I checked Mara too, and she came back human. One of us is lying, and it isn't me.
- **Greta**: Well. Two seers, opposite results on the same person. That's the whole game right there. Let's not rush this.

#### Turn 1
- **Owen**: So one seer's real, one's fake — and the fake is the wolf or the possessed. Tessa, Felix, convince me. Why your read over theirs?
- **Mara**: Obviously Felix is right and Tessa's framing me. She admits she picked me on vibes — "loudest player" is a target of convenience, not evidence.
- **Tessa**: I check the active players, that's where info hides. Felix, you only claimed after me and landed on my exact target. That's a reactive copy.
- **Felix**: Reactive? I held a beat to see if the fake would jump first — and you did. I checked Mara because she steered the table yesterday.
- **Greta**: You both conveniently checked Mara, which is suspiciously tidy. Felix, why her and not Owen or me? Sell me that you actually rolled seer.

#### Turn 2
- **Owen**: My problem, Felix: a real seer who got "human" on Mara would more likely check someone new next, not echo Tessa's exact pick. Mirroring her to clear the wolf is textbook possessed.
- **Mara**: Or Tessa's the wolf, loud-accusing me to wear a bold-seer mask. Don't let Owen tunnel you into his story, Greta.
- **Tessa**: Think it through: a wolf claiming seer and naming a villager gets corrected the instant the real seer speaks. I gave my true result. Felix is shielding you, Mara.
- **Felix**: I picked Mara for the same reason Tessa claims — she was angling to run the flow. The difference is my result is the real one. Lynch Tessa and watch me stay clean.
- **Greta**: I'm not fully sold on Tessa — that "loudest player" logic is thin. But Felix, your timing nags at me more. Second mover, copied target. Leaning Mara, not locked.

#### Turn 3
- **Owen**: I've weighed both. Tessa's claim survives the "why would a wolf ever do this" test; Felix's mirrored result reeks of a possessed save. Take the source, not the gamble. @Mara
- **Mara**: You're all being herded. Felix is the genuine seer and you're about to torch a villager. Fine — I vote the loud accuser. @Tessa
- **Tessa**: My read holds. Mara is the wolf and Felix is covering. End it at the source instead of betting on a seer flip. @Mara
- **Felix**: This is a mistake you can't undo. The real seer dies, the wolf grins. @Tessa
- **Greta**: I genuinely chewed on trusting Felix — but a second mover copying the exact target is the tell that breaks the tie. Remove the accused, not the accuser. @Mara

- Mara -> Tessa
- Tessa -> Mara
