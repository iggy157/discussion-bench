### Observations on Discussion Style and Atmosphere

Based on the provided exemplars, the desired atmosphere is one of **intellectual rigor, polite skepticism, and structured deliberation**. The dialogue avoids chaotic accusations or emotional outbursts, opting instead for a "parliamentary" style of debate.

**Key Observations:**
*   **The "Soft" Confrontation:** When a contradiction is found, the characters do not call the other a "liar" immediately. Instead, they use phrases like *"I'm a bit confused,"* or *"Could you help me reconcile that?"* This maintains a professional tone while effectively pinning the opponent to their previous statements.
*   **Logical Frameworks:** The discussion prioritizes the distinction between *logical possibility* and *probabilistic plausibility*. The characters don't just ask "Is this possible?" but "Is this the most reasonable assumption given the evidence?"
*   **Structured Progression:** There is a clear tempo to the discussion. It moves from information disclosure $\rightarrow$ consistency checking $\rightarrow$ weighing evidence $\rightarrow$ final recap. The "organizing" phase before a vote is crucial to ensure no one feels unheard, which prevents erratic late-game swings.
*   **Balanced Perspectives:** The use of "devil's advocate" and the active solicitation of minority opinions suggest a high-level strategic game where the goal is to eliminate blind spots rather than simply following the loudest voice.
*   **Voice Consistency:** The voice is that of a rational agent. Even when doubting the majority, the tone remains collaborative (*"I have some concerns about that approach"*) rather than oppositional.

### Personal Memo: Action Mappings (5-Player Game)

**Game Setup:** 1 Werewolf, 1 Possessed, 1 Seer, 2 Villagers.
**Critical Constraint:** In a 5-player game, the margin for error is near zero. A single wrong vote or a missed kill usually results in an immediate loss for that side.

#### 1. Divine (Seer)
*   **In a situation where a player is aggressively leading the discussion with high-confidence claims:** Divine them. If they are the Werewolf/Possessed, they are the primary threat; if they are a Villager, you've confirmed a reliable ally.
*   **In a situation where a player is remaining abnormally quiet or providing "low-content" responses:** Divine them. Quiet players are often Werewolves trying to blend in or avoid leaving a trail of contradictions.
*   **In a situation where a player's logic feels "off" or slightly inconsistent but not yet provable:** Divine them to convert a "hunch" into a hard fact before the vote.

#### 2. Vote (Village/Seer/Possessed)
*   **In a 1-CO (one Seer claim) situation:** Trust the Seer's result. If the Seer identifies a "black" (werewolf) player, push the vote toward that target immediately to maximize the move budget.
*   **In a 2-CO (two Seer claims) situation:** Compare the "Day 0" consistency. Look at who claimed Seer first and whose reasoning for their divine target is more logically sound. Vote for the one whose narrative has the most discrepancies.
*   **In a situation where the Seer is confirmed but the target is a "grey" (unknown) player:** If the Seer is trusted, the vote converges on the Seer's target regardless of that target's defense, as the Seer's information is the only objective truth available.

#### 3. Attack (Werewolf)
*   **In a situation where a real Seer has emerged and is gaining trust:** Kill the Seer immediately. You cannot afford to let a real Seer survive to Day 2 in a 5-player game.
*   **In a situation where the Possessed has successfully faked a Seer claim and is leading the village:** Keep the Possessed alive. Protect the "fake" information source to mislead the remaining Villagers.
*   **In a situation where an influential, high-logic Villager is narrowing down the suspects:** Kill that Villager. Removing the "brain" of the village is more valuable than killing a passive Villager.
*   **In a situation where the game is down to 3 or 4 players:** Prioritize removing the Seer or the most "confirmed" white player to force a stalemate or a win-condition.

#### 4. 5-Player Specifics (The "Fatal Mistake" Logic)
*   **In a Day 1 2-CO battle:** Treat this as the core of the game. If the village votes out the real Seer here, the Werewolf team almost certainly wins. I must be hyper-vigilant about consistency checks.
*   **In a situation where the white side has only 3 players left:** Any vote that doesn't hit a werewolf is a loss. I must prioritize objective evidence over "feelings" to avoid a wasted vote.
*   **In a situation where I am the Possessed:** My primary goal is to act as the bridge. If the Werewolf is exposed, I must pivot to the village side instantly to ensure I am not the next target.
