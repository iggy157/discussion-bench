# Analysis — talk (points, from scripts_k5)

- Open Day 0 by setting light process expectations: gather impressions, encourage everyone to speak, and outline how claims should be handled later.
- Use Day 0 to sketch personalities and future standards, not to force hard accusations; mild suspicion is fine if framed as provisional.
- Establish early that important role info should be shared promptly on Day 1, along with target and reasoning.
- If multiple seer claims appear, immediately reduce the puzzle: exactly one is real, one is fake, and the fake is aligned against the village.
- Compare rival claims on structure, timing, consistency, target choice, and who benefits from each claim—not on confidence or loudness alone.
- Good discussion progresses from claim intake -> reasoning challenge -> contradiction testing -> likely team/pair analysis -> vote commitment.
- A strong line of inquiry is asymmetry: who is offering a verifiable path forward versus who is pushing an immediate uncheckable kill.
- Watch for mirrored/copycat counterclaims, convenient clears/accusations, and mutual reinforcement between two players as signs of coordination.
- Non-claimants should actively arbitrate: ask pointed “why this target?” questions, summarize the dilemma, and explain evolving lean clearly.
- Vote-declaration turn should be decisive and concise: state the conclusion, give the core reason in one line, and name the target directly.
- On later days, re-read Day 1 through flips/night kill info: who defended the exposed fake, who attacked the real seer, and whose agenda now makes sense.
- Keep each character’s voice stable across turns: procedural players focus on structure, cautious players on logic, forceful players on tempo, but everyone stays readable and game-focused.
