# Analysis — action (points, from scripts_k5)

- In 5-player games, every move is high-impact; keep decisions simple, early, and tied to win condition rather than long speculation.
- Day 0 action style:
  - Set expectations for Day 1 quickly.
  - Encourage useful information to appear early.
  - Avoid hard commitments from pure vibes.
  - Lightly note who is pushing structure, tempo, or role policy, because those patterns matter later.

- Core Day 1 split: treat **1-CO** and **2-CO** very differently.
  - **If 1-CO seer:** generally center the day around that result unless behavior strongly breaks the claim.
  - **If 2-CO seers:** the seer conflict itself usually becomes the main puzzle; compare claims, timing, target choice, and who benefits.

- In **2-CO**:
  - Assume one is real and one is wolf/possessed.
  - Judge by:
    - who spoke first vs reacted second,
    - whether the second claimant mirrors the first claimant’s target/result structure,
    - whether reasoning for the night target sounds pre-existing or improvised,
    - whether someone is trying to end discussion fast before verification.
  - Mirrored target choice by the later claimant is a major red flag, often read as fake support/cover.
  - Compare not just claim content but the “shape” of alliances that form around each claim.

- When deciding between “lynch the accused target” vs “lynch within the seer conflict”:
  - If a seer gives a black result but the claim is contested, often resolve the seer dispute first unless the black result is overwhelmingly credible.
  - Prefer the safer line that preserves future clarity and avoids handing the fake a free mislynch.

- Behavioral tells that matter in this format:
  - Pushing to rush a kill before another night can verify things.
  - Defending a claim mainly because it benefits you.
  - Steering away from the seer puzzle into an easier villager target.
  - Mutually reinforcing pair behavior between likely wolf/possessed.
  - Overconfident certainty without a strong logic chain.

- Good action logic is explicit and compact:
  - tie your vote/decision to one or two concrete contradictions,
  - prefer “this helps wolf interests” over abstract tone reads,
  - make end-of-day decisions firm and understandable.

- Night/next-day follow-through:
  - If the fake seer/possessed is removed and the real seer dies overnight, use that death as confirmation and re-read Day 1 positions.
  - The likely wolf is often the player who most consistently protected the exposed fake or attacked the real seer/cleared villager.
  - Reconstruct vote and advocacy patterns; in 5p, yesterday’s alignment tells are often enough to finish the game.

- Overall tempo:
  - Open broad, then narrow fast once claims appear.
  - Don’t waste turns repeating setup after the conflict is known.
  - Each utterance/decision should advance: claim, test, compare, commit.
