# Analysis — action (exemplar, from scripts_k5)

### Observations on Exemplar Transcripts

The provided transcripts reveal a highly structured, intellectualized approach to the 5-player AIWolf game. The "good-game" atmosphere is characterized by a meta-game agreement on Day 0, where players establish a shared protocol for information disclosure. This reduces chaotic noise and shifts the battle from "who is shouting" to "whose logic is internally consistent."

**Key Atmospheric & Behavioral Patterns:**
*   **Day 0 Protocol:** Players spend Day 0 not just introducing themselves, but negotiating the "rules of engagement" (e.g., agreeing that the Seer should claim early on Day 1, and that the village should weigh reasoning over volume). This creates a baseline of "expected behavior" that can be used to spot anomalies later.
*   **The "Mirror-Claim" Tell:** A recurring theme in these high-level games is the detection of the Possessed. When two players claim Seer (2-CO), the village looks for "reactive copying." If the second claimant targets the same person as the first but provides a different result, it is often flagged as a "textbook possessed save" or a "mirror-claim" designed to muddy the waters.
*   **Reasoning over Results:** The village does not blindly follow a "Werewolf" result. They analyze *why* the Seer picked that target. A Seer who targets a "loud mover" or someone "steering the flow" is viewed as more credible than one who targets a "quiet player" without a strong justification, as the latter can look like a Wolf framing a convenient scapegoat.
*   **The "Pairing" Logic:** The village actively looks for "mutual-cover" or "tag-teams." If a Seer clears a player and that player immediately becomes the Seer's fiercest defender, the village views this as a potential Wolf-Possessed alliance.
*   **Tempo and Voice:** The dialogue is polite but clinical. Players use phrases like "noting, not accusing" or "my read holds," maintaining a professional distance while applying pressure. The tempo is steady, with a clear transition from general vibes (Day 0) to data analysis (Day 1) to elimination (Day 2).

### Personal Memo: Decision Mappings

#### 1. Divine (Seer)
*   **In the Day 0 night:** Target the most "influential" or "steering" player. If someone is aggressively setting the rules or pushing the tempo, divine them to see if the "engine" of the village is actually the Wolf.
*   **In subsequent nights:** Target the most "unconfirmed" player who is currently gaining trust. If a player is coasting on the "quiet villager" trope or successfully echoing the Seer's reads, they are the highest priority for verification.
*   **When a 2-CO occurs:** If you are the real Seer and a fake has emerged, divine the person the fake is trying to protect or the person who is most vocally supporting the fake.

#### 2. Vote (Village/Seer/Possessed)
*   **In a 1-CO situation (Single Seer claim):** If the Seer provides a "Werewolf" result with sound reasoning, converge the vote on that target immediately. In a 5-player game, the move budget is too thin to "test" a single claim over multiple days.
*   **In a 2-CO situation (Competing Seer claims):**
    *   If the second claimant mirrored the first claimant's target but flipped the result $\rightarrow$ Vote the second claimant (likely Possessed).
    *   If one claimant offers to be tested tomorrow (inviting further divines) while the other demands an immediate lynch $\rightarrow$ Vote the one demanding the immediate lynch.
    *   If one claimant's result creates a "protection pair" (Seer clears X, X defends Seer) $\rightarrow$ Vote the Seer.
*   **On Day 2 (Post-Seer death):** If the Seer was killed after clearing a specific player, that cleared player is the new "anchor." Vote the remaining player who spent Day 1 attacking the true Seer.

#### 3. Attack (Werewolf)
*   **If the Seer claims and is believed:** Kill the Seer immediately. The village cannot afford to lose the Seer's information, but the Wolf cannot afford to let the Seer find the Possessed or the Wolf.
*   **If a 2-CO is successfully muddying the waters:** If the village is split and the Possessed is effectively acting as a shield, consider killing the most influential "neutral" villager who is leaning toward the real Seer.
*   **In the endgame (3 players left):** Kill the only remaining confirmed "human" to force a 50/50 guess or to remove the player most likely to coordinate a correct vote.

#### 4. 5-Player Specifics
*   **The "Fatal Mistake" Rule:** Because the village only needs two correct lynches to win, any "experimental" vote that kills a villager is usually a game-loss. Prioritize certainty over gambles.
*   **The Seer Battle:** Recognize that Day 1 is almost always a battle of authenticity. The goal isn't just to find the Wolf, but to identify which "Seer" is the liability.
*   **Thin Margins:** With only two villagers, the "white side" must act as a single unit. Any deviation in voting patterns between the villagers is a signal the Wolf can exploit to sow discord.
