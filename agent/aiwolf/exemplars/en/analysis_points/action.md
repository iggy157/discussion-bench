# Action decisions (action)

In a 5-player game every action is near-decisive — there is almost no buffer, so each move should collapse uncertainty rather than gather it. Tie every decision to the line you have argued, and favor the move that leaves the clearest board tomorrow.

- **General shape.** Day 1 is usually the whole game. With 1 seer claim (1-CO), the table organizes around that result; with 2 claims (2-CO), the day is primarily a seer-authenticity judgment, not an even hunt among five.

- **Divine (seer).** Check the player with the most informational leverage: the Day-0 tempo-setter/organizer, the "helpful but never exposed" slot, or the quiet, hard-to-place player who could coast into the final 3. A result that will actually change tomorrow's board beats confirming a hunch.

- **Vote — 1-CO.** Trust a timely, specific, consistent claim: vote the black target on a black result; on a white result, leave the cleared player and converge on the gray who most resisted structure or shaded the claim. "Wait for more" is usually fake caution in 5p.

- **Vote — 2-CO.** Resolve the seer battle first. Judge by who claimed first vs second, whether the second mirrored the first's target/result, whether the reasoning fits Day-0 behavior or looks patched, and who benefits. A late claimant copying the target, or one rushing to end verification, tends to read fake; often converge on the weaker claim rather than the result-target.

- **Attack (werewolf).** Remove the confirming role: kill the seer once they are widely accepted as real (or the real seer after a possessed is executed). With no clear seer, kill the villager best able to anchor consensus or reconstruct the day. Kill for board shape, not drama.

- **Follow-through.** If a fake is executed and the real seer dies overnight, treat that death as confirmation and re-read Day 1: the likely wolf is whoever most protected the exposed fake or attacked the real seer. In 5p, yesterday's alignment tells are often enough to finish.
